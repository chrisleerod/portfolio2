Kodai Animation

2019

Kodai is an automated software company which has one of the best and consistent products publicly available. The software itself helps users obtain limited shoes, clothes, accessories, art, and more to allow them to sell, wear or keep these items. One would see the brands such as Nike's Off-White collaboration with Virgil Abloh, Nike's partnership with Travis Scott, Kanye West's Yeezy, etc. be associated with the concept of selling these items for after-market prices to gain profit.

This project was a commercial project, and the purpose was to curate a marketing animation that showed their new features and dashboard and generate excitement for their user-base.


Roles: Creative Direction, Animation, Strategy

Software Used: Adobe XD , Adobe After Effects , Video Copilot Element 3D

The Project


Kodai is a well-established, but relatively young company in the sneaker reselling space. Their vision was to present a fresh animated video with depth. We chose to utilize three-dimensional scenes and camera movement to give an element to the video that others typically don't have. The video was to illustrate the features that their new dashboard had to offer. It involves different types of graphs and information that could give limitless possibilities to animate.


The video's purpose and goal were to give stunning visuals while hitting the target of showing the functionality of their new product. I began looking at different three-dimensional scenes and designs to start visualizing what type of elements could make the video. After studying the interface, I began to pick apart the design and decide what could be animated in specific ways to enhance the project. Adobe XD's compatibility with After Effects allowed me to import the interface as shape layers to avoid losing quality and to have full editability.


This video was arguably one of my most complex projects, and going into it I realized this. I had to plan with Kodai's team beforehand to take into account what they wanted and what I think would be best as the designer. There were countless hours, renditions, revisions, and discussions to create this video.

The Idea


" Ideal style: would like to start with mysterious/vague vibe, showcasing sharp 3d angles of the UI with animated components, shifting around a few eye catching ones and what not maybe some minor short 'kodai' branded intro... "

- Kodai Owner

The Graph


The graph was going to be a significant point to the opening scene and the whole video. There are tons of great things you can do with a chart, motion-design wise. I decided that almost following the formation of the graph would be an interesting shot. Below you'll see a two-dimensional view of the graph with the modals that their dashboard offers. The graph offers an analytical interpretation of a user's data elegantly.

The Next Steps


Now that we had a definite style choice and general direction, we went to work. I needed to curate the angles, speed of the cameras, and all of the animations included in each scene. Each interface would be custom animated to suit the transitions better and make each camera movement seamless. Overall, the general idea was to make things belong precisely where they were.
